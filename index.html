Climate Hotspots: A Sustainability Index for Agricultural Production


The world is facing unprecedented challenges due to climate change, which threatens the long-term sustainability of our food systems. The impacts of climate change are already being felt around the world, but the magnitude and distribution of these impacts are not evenly spread. Mapping the world’s climate hotspots and identifying where the impacts will be the greatest is increasingly important for governments, advocacy groups, and others who need to prioritize resources, set goals, and adapt to a warming world.

Our website focuses on developing an index of agricultural vulnerability to climate change, which computes the potential damage to agricultural productivity under different climate change scenarios in the period between 2070-2100. The resulting sustainability index is shown in maps which are categorized based on three distinct climate scenarios: RCP 2.6, RCP 6.0, and RCP 8.5. See the Documentation section or map description for details.

We aim to provide a comprehensive assessment of the impact of climate change on global agriculture, using state-of-the-art analytical tools. Our index can be used to identify areas that are particularly vulnerable to climate change, and can be used to prioritize adaptation efforts and allocate resources accordingly. At the same time we hope that this project can contribute to understanding the devastating impact that climate change will have locally and the unequal distribution of its damages.

We hope that this website will serve as a valuable resource for researchers, policymakers, and anyone who is interested in understanding the impact of climate change on agriculture. We invite you to explore our website and learn more about our work.
